# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Developers inspecting Ethscription content delivery behavior in browser contexts. They are checking how raw on-chain bytes differ from web-embeddable asset responses.

## Product Purpose

Show why gzipped on-chain font payloads do not work directly in `@font-face`, and why a decoded `/content` endpoint is useful for browsers while `/data` remains canonical raw data.

## Brand Personality

Technical, direct, forensic. The interface should feel like a small lab bench for confirming browser behavior, not a marketing page.

## Anti-references

No generic SaaS hero treatment, no decorative gradients, no fake metrics, no oversized cards, no glass UI, no vague product copy.

## Design Principles

- Show the byte-level distinction plainly.
- Keep the side-by-side comparison visible without extra interaction.
- Make failure and success legible without hiding the broken case.
- Keep copy short enough for a developer to scan during debugging.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Use high contrast text, semantic sections, readable labels, and responsive stacking for narrow screens. Avoid motion that is not tied to state.
